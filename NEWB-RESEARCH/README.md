## Beginner-Friendly Edges (No Technical Skills)

### 1. Personal Expertise Edge
> "Your existing knowledge is your initial advantage"

- Trade topics you already follow (sports, politics, economics)
- Filter noise vs signal (viral tweets vs actual data)
- Example: Economics student bet on rate hikes when market underpriced them

### 2. Market Psychology Edge
> "Predict how others will behave, not just what will happen"

- Liquidity and incentives drive odds, not just information
- Emotional trading creates opportunities
- Reddit success story: $40k profit understanding behavioral patterns

### 3. Time-Based Arbitrage
- **Early betting**: Softer odds, beat closing line
- **Late betting**: Maximum information (injuries, weather)
- Example: Movie box office - bought at panic price after reviews

### 4. The 2% Rule
- Never risk more than 2% of total capital per position
- Protects from variance and bad runs
- Start with $20-50 to learn

## How to Explain Prediction Markets (Simply)

### Most Effective Analogies

1. **"Stock Market for Events"** (Most Common)
   - Instead of trading company shares, you trade outcomes
   - Buy "Yes" or "No" shares in future events

2. **"Wisdom of Crowds"**
   - Like asking a crowd to guess jelly beans in a jar
   - Average guess is often surprisingly accurate

3. **"Betting on the Weather"**
   - If many people bet on rain, the "price" goes up
   - Price reflects perceived probability

### Price as Probability (Core Concept)

- $0.65 = 65% chance of happening
- $0.10 = 10% chance (very unlikely)
- If you think real probability is HIGHER than price, buy "Yes"
- If you think real probability is LOWER than price, buy "No"

### Binary Options Explained

- Contracts pay $1 if correct, $0 if wrong
- Buy at $0.65 â†’ if correct, profit = $0.35 ($1.00 - $0.65)
- Maximum loss = purchase price (can't lose more than you invest)

## Common Beginner Questions

### "Is this gambling?"

**Answer**: It's informed speculation, not casino gambling.

| Prediction Markets | Betting Markets |
|-------------------|-----------------|
| Trade against other traders | Trade against the house |
| Market-driven odds | House sets odds with margin |
| Price discovery focus | Entertainment focus |

### "How do I make money?"

1. Buy low, sell high (like stocks)
2. Hold until resolution (event happens)
3. Start with $20-50 to learn
4. Research events before trading

### "Can I lose more than I invest?"

**No**. Maximum loss = what you paid for shares. Binary options have capped risk ($0 or $1).

## Recommended Learning Path

### Step 1: Risk-Free Practice (Weeks 1-2)
- **Platform**: Metaculus or Manifold
- **Budget**: Play money (zero risk)
- **Focus**: Learn mechanics, observe markets
- **Action**: Make 10-20 small predictions

### Step 2: Real Money, Small Stakes (Weeks 3-4)
- **Platform**: Kalshi (US) or Polymarket (crypto)
- **Budget**: $20-50
- **Focus**: Trade topics you know well
- **Action**: Apply 2% rule, track results

### Step 3: Expand Gradually
- **Budget**: Increase only if profitable
- **Focus**: Refine your edge (expertise areas)
- **Action**: Diversify across markets
